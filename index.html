How to maintain your mental health on self-isolation

If you are reading this, most likely you are stuck on self-isolation and must stay at home for at least 14 days

  • without shopping 👠
  • without seeing your friends 👯
  • without bars 🍻
  • without chilling in McDonalds 🍟
  • without possibility to feed ducks in the park 😢

No worries! Let us share a short and valuable tip list that will help you not to loose yourself and stay mentally and physically healthy!

Dealing with anxiety during self-isolation may prove to be hard. The person focuses on negative news, is constantly worried for their children and their relatives, suffers from sleep disorders, and other manifestations of anxiety. Self-care and care for others will help you to stay sane during self-isolation: in a confined space, it is highly important to take care of your physical and psychological state.

If you don't know what is self isolation, cilck HERE!

So what should you do not to go crazy?

  1. Do sports, your body needs regular exercise. Short walks from the refrigerator to the living room don't count. 🏋
  2. Drink plenty of water. If you think you are drinking enough water, you are not. Add at least one more glass to your daily routine. 💧
  3. Breathe some fresh air. If you don't have a private garden or something, ventilate your apartment. 🌬
  4. Work on relationships with your loved ones. How long have you not talked to your parents, grandmother, uncle, cousin, or that sweet bakery lady? This may be the best time to call or write them to say: "Hi, I miss you." 💕
  5. Find your best way of meditation. It may be listening to the sounds of the nature on Youtube or watching funny sitcoms on Netflix, knitting an eco-friendly shopper bag or building bridges from the chopsticks you get ordering asian food. 🥢
  6. Play with your pet. If you still don't have any, ask your friends to get you one! Maybe somewhere on the other side of the town a lovely cat, dog, owl, lobster, goldfish or tarantula is waiting to become you best friend. 🦉
